# Governance

OMBS is a community-stewarded standard. This document describes how decisions are made.

## Stewardship model

OMBS is currently in **bootstrap stewardship** — maintained by the original drafting team while community structures are built. The intent is to transition to a multi-stakeholder steering committee within 12 months of v1.0 release, with seats reserved for: practicing educators (≥3), researchers in learning sciences (≥2), ed-tech implementers (≥2), parent/learner representatives (≥1), and an independent chair.

## Decision types

| Decision | Process | Quorum |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial (typos, clarity) | Maintainer merge | 1 maintainer |
| Crosswalk additions | Maintainer merge after 7-day comment | 1 maintainer + no objections |
| Evidence descriptor changes | Steering review | Simple majority |
| New dimensions / codes | Steering review + 30-day public comment | 2/3 majority |
| Major version (breaking) | Steering review + 60-day public comment + migration plan | 2/3 majority |
| License change | Unanimous steering + community vote | Per CC license terms |

## Conflict of interest

Steering members must disclose: employment with curriculum publishers, ed-tech vendors building on OMBS, assessment companies, or research grants tied to OMBS adoption. A member with a material COI on a specific decision recuses from voting on that decision.

## Forks

OMBS is CC BY-SA. Forks are permitted and encouraged when reasonable consensus cannot be reached. Forks must use a distinct name (see LICENSE.md).

## Sunset

If the steering committee dissolves and no successor is named within 6 months, the latest stable version becomes the canonical reference indefinitely. The CC BY-SA license ensures the standard remains usable regardless of organizational continuity.
